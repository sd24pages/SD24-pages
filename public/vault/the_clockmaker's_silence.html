The Clockmaker's Silence

Every second in Elias’s shop was accounted for. Three hundred clocks lined the walls, a symphony of rhythmic ticking that most visitors found maddening, but Elias found it to be the only thing that made sense.

He didn't just fix gears; he listened to the heartbeat of time itself. But this morning, the rhythm had changed. One clock, a heavy oak grandfather piece in the corner, had stopped dead at 4:12 AM.

It wasn't the broken spring that bothered him. It was the fact that as soon as that clock stopped, the wind outside ceased to blow, and the birds in the eaves went silent. Elias reached for his jeweler’s loupe, his hands trembling slightly.

He realized then that he wasn't just repairing a clock—he was holding the rest of the world’s afternoon in his hands. If he didn't move the gear now, tomorrow might never arrive.
